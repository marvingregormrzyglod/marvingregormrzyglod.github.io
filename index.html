Game Developer,
Systems Designer
& Bible Nerd

Marvin Gregor Mrzyglod has developed blockbuster video games, pioneered practical use of Self-Determination Theory and was entrusted with design leadership of Sony's oldest exclusive PlayStation franchise. However, he quit gaming to develop new ways of interacting with the Bible and experience scripture in refreshing ways.

He has worked with a range of different languages and tools. The things he currently has the most experience with are: LUA, JavaScript, HTML, CSS, git, Unreal Engine, Anvil/Scimitar, GameMaker, GML Script and Eschatology.

Game Development Experience

Design Consultant

Sony Interactive Entertainment | May 2022 - October 2022

Sony reached out to me, and requested my expertise in data-driven design techniques for conceiving game mechanics based on [Self-Determination Theory](https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/theory/) for an untitled multiplayer game (beloved PlayStation franchise).

Lead Designer

Lucid Games | November 2020 - February 2023

Led the game design, level design and narrative design teams, total of 11 designers. Conceived core gameplay vision. Designed vehicle handling (unique, never before seen vehicle traversal mechanics). Designed 3rd-person over-the-shoulder shooting gameplay (reticle behavior, camera tweens, the works). Blocked out multiplayer arenas and large open-world city map in Unreal Engine 5. Wrote character backstories and barks. Interviewed and hired two new designers. Pioneered data-driven design and produced competitive intelligence reports (interpretation of sales data, focus groups, A/B tests and telemetrics).

Education

Rational Game Design (RGD)

Concordia Univerity | 2019

This course is known as Ubisoft Game Creator Odyssey Part I and based on 30 years of game design history and teaches Rational Game Design, which presents methods for conceiving game mechanics rationally (without relative subjectivity) to let designers manipulate data to reliably keep players in a flow-state.

Rational Level Design (RLD)

Concordia Univerity | 2019

This course is known as Ubisoft Game Creator Odyssey Part II and teaches how to apply Rational Game Design (RGD) methods on level design problems.
